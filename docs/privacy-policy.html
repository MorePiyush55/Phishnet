Privacy Policy

Last Updated: September 24, 2025

1. Introduction

PhishNet ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our email security scanning service.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Personal Information

  • Google Account Information: Email address, name, and profile picture from your Google account
  • Gmail Data: Read-only access to your Gmail messages for security analysis
  • Usage Data: Information about how you interact with our service

2.2 Gmail Data Processing

We access your Gmail data solely for security analysis purposes:

  • Email headers and metadata for threat detection
  • Sender information and email content for phishing analysis
  • Links and attachments for malware scanning
  • Email patterns for security assessment

3. How We Use Your Information

3.1 Primary Uses

  • Security Analysis: Detect phishing attempts, malware, and other email threats
  • Threat Protection: Provide real-time security assessments of your emails
  • Service Operation: Maintain and improve our security detection capabilities
  • User Authentication: Verify your identity and maintain secure sessions

3.2 Data Processing Principles

  • Real-time Processing: Gmail data is analyzed in real-time and not permanently stored
  • Security Focus: Data processing is limited to security analysis only
  • No Modification: We never modify, delete, or send emails on your behalf
  • User Control: You can revoke access at any time

4. Data Storage and Security

4.1 Security Measures

  • Encryption: All OAuth tokens encrypted at rest using industry-standard encryption
  • Secure Transmission: All data transmitted over HTTPS/TLS
  • Access Controls: Strict per-user data isolation
  • Token Management: Secure token storage and automatic expiration

4.2 Data Retention

  • Gmail Data: Not permanently stored; processed in real-time only
  • OAuth Tokens: Stored securely until you revoke access
  • User Profile: Basic profile information retained while account is active
  • Analysis Results: Security analysis summaries may be temporarily cached

5. Data Sharing

5.1 No Third-Party Sharing

We do not sell, trade, or otherwise transfer your Gmail data to third parties. Your email data is processed solely by our security analysis systems.

5.2 Service Providers

We may use trusted service providers for:

  • Cloud hosting and infrastructure (with appropriate data protection agreements)
  • Security monitoring and threat intelligence
  • Technical support and maintenance

6. Your Rights and Controls

6.1 Access Control

  • Revoke Access: Remove PhishNet's access through your Google Account settings
  • Data Access: Request information about data we've processed
  • Account Deletion: Delete your PhishNet account and associated data
  • Opt-out: Discontinue use of our service at any time

6.2 Google Account Settings

You can manage PhishNet's access to your Google account at: https://myaccount.google.com/permissions

7. Legal Basis for Processing

We process your data based on:

  • Consent: You provide explicit consent through Google OAuth
  • Legitimate Interest: Providing email security services you requested
  • Service Performance: Processing necessary to perform our security analysis service

8. International Data Transfers

Your data may be processed in countries outside your residence. We ensure appropriate safeguards are in place to protect your data according to applicable privacy laws.

9. Changes to Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. We will notify you of significant changes through our service or via email. Your continued use after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

10. Children's Privacy

Our service is not intended for users under 18 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 18.

11. Compliance

This Privacy Policy complies with:

  • Google API Services User Data Policy
  • General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
  • California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)
  • Other applicable privacy laws
